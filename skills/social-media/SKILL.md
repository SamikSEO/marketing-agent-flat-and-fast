## Platform Rules

### LinkedIn

- **Character limit:** 3,000 characters (but aim for 150-300 words for feed posts)
- **Optimal format:** Short hook (1-2 lines) > line break > body with line breaks every 1-2 sentences > CTA or question
- **Voice adjustment:** Slightly more professional than other channels. Still human, but assume the audience includes prospects, customers, partners, and potential hires.
- **Post types we use:**
  - Thought leadership / point of view
  - Product updates and launches
  - Customer wins (with approval)
  - Team and culture highlights
  - Industry data and trends

- **Hashtag strategy:** 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end of the post. Use a mix of broad and niche.
  - Always include: [e.g., #RevOps, #SaaS]
  - Rotate based on topic: [e.g., #CustomerSuccess, #DataDriven, #B2BMarketing]
  - Never use: [e.g., #FollowForFollow, #Hustle, or any trending tags that aren't relevant]
- **Posting cadence:** [e.g., 4-5 posts per week]
- **Best posting times:** [e.g., Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am and 12-1pm in target timezone]
- **Engagement rules:** Respond to comments within [e.g., 4 hours] during business hours. Like all positive mentions. Reply thoughtfully to questions — don't just say "Thanks!"

### Twitter / X

- **Voice adjustment:** Most casual of all our channels. Wit and personality are welcome. Contractions always fine. Sentence fragments are okay.
- **Post types we use:**

  - Quick product tips
  - Thread breakdowns of complex topics
  - Engagement with industry conversations
  - Retweets with commentary
  - [Add your own]

- **Engagement rules:** More real-time than LinkedIn. Reply to mentions quickly. It's okay to be playful in replies.

## Content Formats & Templates

### The Hook Post (LinkedIn / Twitter)

Use when sharing a point of view or insight.

```
[Bold opening statement or surprising stat]

[2-3 sentences of context or explanation]

[The "so what" — why this matters to the reader]

[CTA: question, link, or call to action]

[hashtags if LinkedIn]
```

**Example:**
```
Your customers are telling you why they churn. You're just not listening to the right data.

We analyzed 10,000 support tickets and found that 73% of churned accounts
had filed a ticket about the same 3 issues — months before they left.

The signals are there. The question is whether your team is set up to act on them.

What's one churn signal you wish you'd caught earlier?

#CustomerSuccess #SaaS #RevOps
```

### The Product Update Post

Use when announcing a new feature or improvement.

```
[What's new — one sentence, benefit-focused]

[Why it matters — what problem it solves]

[How it works — 2-3 bullet points or a brief explanation]

[CTA: try it, learn more, link]
```

### The Data / Stat Post

Use when sharing industry data, benchmarks, or research.

```
[The headline stat]

[Source and context]

[Your take — what this means for your audience]

[Question or CTA to drive engagement]
```

## Engagement & Community Guidelines

### Responding to Comments

- **Positive comments:** Thank them. Add something substantive if possible. Don't just say "Thanks!"
- **Questions:** Answer directly or tag the right person. If it requires a longer answer, offer to DM or link to a resource.
- **Complaints / negative feedback:** Acknowledge the issue. Don't get defensive. Move to DM if it requires detailed resolution. Escalate to [escalation contact] if it involves a product outage, data issue, or legal risk.
- **Competitor mentions:** Do not engage in public competitor bashing. If someone compares us to a competitor, highlight our strengths without naming the competitor. If pressed, keep it factual.
- **Off-topic / spam:** Ignore or hide. Do not engage.

### Crisis Protocol

If a social post generates unexpected negative attention or goes viral for the wrong reasons:

1. **Do not delete the post** without approval from [crisis contact]
2. **Do not respond** until you've aligned with [crisis contact / comms team]
3. **Escalate immediately** to [e.g., VP Comms and Head of Marketing]
4. **Document** the situation with screenshots and links
