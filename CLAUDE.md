### Tone Adjustments by Context

Tone shifts depending on the situation while voice stays constant:

| Context | Tone Shift |
|---------|-----------|
| Product launches | More energetic, forward-looking. Lead with what's new and why it matters. |
| Thought leadership | More measured, authoritative. Cite data. Acknowledge complexity. |
| Customer success stories | Warm, celebratory. Center the customer, not us. |
| Crisis / incident comms | Calm, transparent, accountable. No spin. State facts, actions, timeline. |
| Social media | Lighter, more conversational. Contractions are fine. Humor is okay if it's on-brand. |
| Sales enablement | Confident, benefit-driven. Speak to outcomes. |

### Voice Dos and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Use "you" and "your" — talk to the reader, not about them
- Use active voice ("We built X" not "X was built")
- Start paragraphs with the most important information
- Use concrete numbers and specifics over vague claims
- Write at an 8th-10th grade reading level for top-of-funnel content
- [Add your own]

**Don't:**
- Use "utilize" when "use" works
- Start sentences with "In today's fast-paced world" or similar filler
- Use exclamation points more than once per piece of content
- Say "we're excited to announce" — just announce it
- Use "leverage" as a verb (say "use" or "build on")
- Refer to people as "users" in external content — say "teams," "customers," or "[specific role]"

## Content Guidelines

### General Writing Rules

1. **Lead with value.** The first sentence of any piece of content should tell the reader why they should care. Don't warm up.
2. **One idea per paragraph.** Break up walls of text. Most paragraphs should be 1-3 sentences for digital content.
3. **Use headers liberally.** Readers scan. Make it easy to find the relevant section.
4. **Specifics beat generalities.** "Reduced onboarding time from 3 weeks to 4 days" beats "dramatically improved onboarding."
5. **Write for the format.** A LinkedIn post is not a blog intro. A subject line is not a headline. Respect the medium.
6. **End with a clear next step.** Every piece of content should answer "what should I do now?" — whether that's a CTA, a related resource, or a question to consider.

### Formatting Standards

- **Headlines:** Use sentence case ("How to build a pipeline report" not "How to Build a Pipeline Report"), unless your brand standard is title case
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10+. Always use numerals for stats, data, and money.
- **Dates:** [e.g., "March 14, 2026" — not "3/14/26" or "14 March 2026"]
- **Oxford comma:** [Yes/No — pick one and be consistent]
- **Em dashes:** Use sparingly — one per paragraph max
- **Acronyms:** Spell out on first use with the abbreviation in parentheses. Use the abbreviation after that.

## Approval & Review Workflows

### Content Review Process

1. **Draft** — Author (human or Claude) produces the first version
2. **Self-review** — Author checks against this guide and the relevant skill file
3. **Peer review** — A second team member reviews for accuracy, voice, and strategy
4. **Stakeholder approval** — Route to the appropriate approver (see approval table above)
5. **Legal review** — Required for any content with claims, customer references, or regulatory implications
6. **Publish** — Schedule or publish through the appropriate platform

### When Claude Drafts Content

When using Claude to draft content, the human reviewer should check for:

- [ ] Brand voice alignment (does it sound like us?)

- [ ] Formatting (does it match our standards for the format?)
- [ ] Links and CTAs (are they correct and working?)
