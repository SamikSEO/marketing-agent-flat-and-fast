## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

**Company Name:** CertCheck
**Website:** https://www.certcheck.in/
**Category:** Blockchain-powered credential verification & trust infrastructure
**Stage:** Early-growth SaaS (India-first, enterprise and institutional focus)
**Tagline:** "Power Your Certificate Verification Process with Confidence"

**What CertCheck Does — In One Line:**
CertCheck is a blockchain-based platform that lets institutions issue tamper-proof digital certificates and lets employers, recruiters, and verifiers instantly authenticate those credentials — without phone calls, manual paperwork, or fraud risk.

**Core Value Proposition:**
- Every credential is anchored on blockchain → immutable, timestamped, auditable
- Real-time verification (vs. days-long manual BGV turnaround)
- Three-sided ecosystem: Issuers (universities/institutions) → Certificates → Verifiers (employers/BGV firms)
- Issuers earn revenue per successful verification; verifiers pay only for what they validate
- Reduces time-to-hire by up to 80%
- Eliminates manual fraud risk from the credential lifecycle entirely

Establish CertCheck as **the default trust layer for credential verification in India** — the infrastructure layer that sits between institutions that issue certificates and the employers/regulators who need to trust them.

### Growth Goals (Content & SEO Lens)
1. **Capture demand** from HR, talent acquisition, and compliance teams actively searching for BGV and credential verification solutions
2. **Build topical authority** around: blockchain credentialing, hiring fraud, fake degree prevention, BGV automation, enterprise verification, regulatory compliance

## 8. CONTENT DO'S AND DON'TS

### DO
- Anchor every article in a real, recent, verifiable event or dataset
- Use India-specific data wherever possible (RBI, SEBI, Ministry of Labour context when relevant)
- Reference real competitors by name when educationally appropriate (not disparagingly)
- Write FAQs based on actual search queries (from keyword data)
- Position CertCheck as the category solution, not the only solution
- End every blog with a soft CTA (demo, connect, learn more)

### DON'T
- Make unverifiable claims about CertCheck's own metrics without internal sign-off
- Reproduce exact statistics without citing the original source
- Use AI-sounding phrases that fail the "would a senior journalist write this?" test
- Write about CertCheck features in a product-sheet tone inside editorial content
- Target keywords outside CertCheck's actual service area (e.g., pure KYC/AML content belongs to IDfy territory)

## 11. THINGS TO ALWAYS REMEMBER

1. **CertCheck is not a BGV company.** It is a blockchain credentialing infrastructure company. This distinction matters for ICP targeting, SEO keyword selection, and competitive positioning.
2. **The two-sided marketplace model is unique.** Issuers earn; verifiers pay per check. This should come through in institutional and enterprise content.
3. **India context is non-negotiable.** Every article should feel written for India's hiring and compliance environment — not adapted from a US/UK piece.
4. **The competitor AuthBridge already owns the enterprise BGV conversation.** CertCheck should not fight on that turf. Own "blockchain certificate verification" and let that expand into the broader BGV conversation over time.
5. **Topical authority before volume.** Better to own "blockchain credential verification India" deeply than to scatter across all HR topics. Focus first, expand later.
6. **Every piece of content is a trust signal.** CertCheck sells trust. Its content must itself be trustworthy — cited, accurate, honest, and editorially credible.
